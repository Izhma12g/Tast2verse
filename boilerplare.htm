Authentic Italian Pizza

Ingredients List

  • 500g all-purpose flour
  • 325ml warm water
  • 1 packet active dry yeast
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 100kg Chopped pineapple
  • 200g Sliced mozzarella cheese
  • 150g tomato sauce
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • 1 teaspoon oregano
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon pepper flakes
  • 100g sliced pepperoni

Making authentic Italian pizza is a delightful and rewarding experience, perfect for bringing a touch of Italy into your home. Begin by combining the warm water and sugar in a small bowl, then sprinkle the yeast on top and let it sit for about 10 minutes until frothy. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Create a well in the centre and pour in the yeast mixture along with the olive oil. Stir until a dough forms. Knead the dough on a floured surface for about 10 minutes until smooth and elastic. Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1 hour, or until it has doubled in size.

Once the dough has risen, preheat your oven to its highest setting, ideally around 250°C (480°F). Punch down the dough and divide it into two equal parts. Roll each part into a ball and let them rest for another 10 minutes. On a floured surface, roll out one of the dough balls into a thin circle, about 30cm (12 inches) in diameter. Transfer the dough to a baking sheet or pizza stone. Spread a thin layer of tomato sauce over the dough, leaving a small border around the edges. Sprinkle the oregano and garlic powder evenly over the sauce. Arrange the mozzarella cheese slices on top, followed by any additional toppings you desire, such as pepperoni or fresh basil leaves.

Bake the pizza in the preheated oven for about 10-12 minutes, or until the crust is beautifully golden and the cheese is bubbling and lightly browned. If you're using a pizza stone, the baking time might be a little shorter due to the even distribution of heat. Keep an eye on it to avoid burning. Once baked to perfection, carefully remove the pizza from the oven and allow it to cool for a minute or two to let the cheese set slightly, making it easier to slice.

Before serving, you can add an extra touch of flavour by garnishing the pizza with fresh basil leaves, a drizzle of high-quality olive oil, or a sprinkle of chilli flakes for a bit of heat. For a truly authentic Italian experience, pair your pizza with a simple green salad and a glass of your favourite wine. Repeat the rolling, topping, and baking process with the second dough ball.

Now, enjoy your delicious homemade authentic Italian pizza, savouring each bite of the crispy crust, rich tomato sauce, and melted mozzarella cheese! Buon appetito!
